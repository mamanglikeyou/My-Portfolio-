## Overview

High employee attrition rates pose a serious challenge for large organizations. The **Employee Attrition Prediction & Analytics** project was developed to help an Edutech company tackle a turnover rate exceeding 10% (reaching 16.9%). This project combines Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA), Machine Learning, and Business Intelligence to identify the root causes of employee turnover and predict individuals at high risk of leaving.

## Business Problem & Scope

The core issues faced by the company were:
- Unacceptably high attrition rates leading to increased recruitment costs.
- Unidentified key factors causing employees to resign.
- A lack of a predictive system, forcing HR to be reactive rather than proactive.
- Absence of real-time visual monitoring tools.

To solve this, the project scope included deep-dive EDA, developing a **Random Forest Classifier** prediction model, building an interactive **Looker Studio Dashboard**, and providing actionable business recommendations.

## Machine Learning Approach

A **Random Forest Classifier** was trained and tuned using `RandomizedSearchCV`, adjusting for class imbalance with `class_weight='balanced'`. 

The model achieved strong and reliable performance:
- **Accuracy**: 82.08%
- **AUC-ROC**: 0.7724, indicating good separation capability between leaving and staying employees.
- **Cross-Validation F1-Score**: 0.4788 (±0.0307), confirming stability and lack of severe overfitting.

A custom `prediction.py` script was also created to allow HR to input sample employee data and instantly receive an attrition risk score (Low, Medium, High).

## Feature Importance Insights

Analysis of the Random Forest feature importances revealed the primary drivers of attrition:
1. **Workload**: `OverTime` was the #1 predictor. Employees working frequent overtime had an attrition rate near 30%, compared to ~10% for others.
2. **Compensation & Benefits**: Variables like `StockOptionLevel`, `MonthlyIncome`, and `DailyRate` heavily influenced resignations, proving that competitive financial packages are crucial.
3. **Demographics & Career Path**: `Age` (younger employees 18-30), `TotalWorkingYears`, and `JobLevel` showed that entry-level, younger staff are highly vulnerable to leaving if career paths are unclear.

## Business Dashboard

To facilitate continuous monitoring, an interactive dashboard was built using **Google Looker Studio**. The dashboard translates complex data into clear visual insights, including:
- Real-time overall attrition rates.
- Job roles with the highest turnover (e.g., Sales Representatives, Laboratory Technicians).
- Visual breakdowns of the relationship between overtime, income, and attrition.

## Actionable Recommendations for HR

Based on the data, several strategic interventions were recommended:
- **Strictly Manage Overtime**: Since overtime is the strongest predictor of attrition, HR must implement workload redistribution and cap excessive overtime hours.
- **Adjust Compensation**: Provide targeted salary increases, retention bonuses, or better stock options for high-risk roles.
- **Develop Young Talent**: Create structured mentoring and clear career development plans specifically aimed at employees aged 18-30.
- **Deploy Predictive Interventions**: Utilize the deployed Machine Learning script to regularly score employee risk and proactively engage those flagged as HIGH or MEDIUM risk.
