## Overview

Student attrition is a major concern for educational institutions, impacting both reputation and revenue. The **Student Dropout Prediction & Risk Analytics** project was developed to help Jaya Jaya Institut tackle a critical dropout rate of 32.1%. By utilizing **Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)**, **Machine Learning**, and **Business Intelligence dashboards**, this project provides a robust early warning system to identify at-risk students before they leave.

## Business Problem & Scope

The primary challenges faced by the institution included:

- A high dropout rate despite a generally good academic reputation.
- The absence of an early detection mechanism, leading to delayed academic interventions.
- A lack of clear understanding regarding the primary factors influencing a student's decision to drop out.

To address this, the project involved analyzing a comprehensive dataset (4424 records, 37 features) covering demographics, family background, finances, and academic performance. The solution includes a **Random Forest Classifier** model and an interactive **Streamlit prototype** for online predictions.

## Data Insights & Feature Importance

Through data analysis and machine learning feature importance extraction, several key drivers of student dropouts were identified:

1. **Academic Performance is Paramount**: The number of approved courses and grades achieved in the 1st and 2nd semesters were the strongest predictors of dropout. Students struggling early on are at the highest risk.
2. **Financial Stability**: Financial factors play a significant role. Students whose tuition fees are not up to date have a vastly higher dropout rate.
3. **Scholarships as a Shield**: Holding a scholarship significantly reduces the likelihood of dropping out, providing a crucial safety net for vulnerable students.
4. **Age at Enrollment**: Students enrolling at an older age tend to have higher dropout risks, likely due to balancing external responsibilities like work or family.

## Machine Learning & Deployment

A **Random Forest Classifier** was trained to predict whether a student will Graduate, Dropout, or remain Enrolled. To handle class imbalances, the model was tuned using `RandomizedSearchCV` and the `class_weight='balanced'` parameter.
The finalized model and its `StandardScaler` were exported as joblib artifacts and integrated into a **Streamlit** web application. This application allows users to input student profiles and instantly receive a probability score and risk assessment.

The model achieved excellent performance metrics:

- **Accuracy (93.11%)**: Successfully classified the vast majority of students into the correct categories.
- **F1-Score (91.17%)**: Demonstrated a strong balance between Precision and Recall, minimizing false predictions.
- **AUC-ROC (97.21%)**: Showed outstanding capability in distinguishing between potential dropouts and safe students.
- **CV F1-Score (87.62% ±1.63%)**: Cross-validation confirmed the model is stable and not overfitting.

## Business Dashboard

An interactive dashboard was built using **Google Looker Studio** to monitor student performance comprehensively. It features:

- High-level KPIs including total students, dropout rates, and graduate rates.
- Detailed bar charts breaking down dropout rates by study program, scholarship status, and average approved courses.

**Dashboard Link**: [Google Looker Studio Dashboard](https://datastudio.google.com/reporting/4682a376-05ad-405f-8818-17f7cb81f36b)

## Actionable Recommendations

Based on the findings, the following strategies were recommended for the institution:

- **Deploy Early Warning Systems**: Utilize the Streamlit prototype to flag at-risk students right from the first semester using early academic data.
- **Intensive Academic Mentoring**: Provide immediate academic support or tutorials for students with low approved course counts in their initial semesters.
- **Expand Financial Aid**: Increase scholarship availability and create flexible payment plans, as tuition arrears strongly correlate with dropping out.
- **Support for Older Students**: Offer flexible scheduling or remote learning options for older enrollees who may be juggling multiple responsibilities.
